What this is

This document is the single source of truth for the LBT Technology Resilience commercial workflow — from the moment a prospect lands on the lite assessment on our website, through to a signed managed service agreement and an ongoing quarterly review cycle.

Every step is documented with: who owns it, what triggers it, what systems are involved, and what gaps still need to be built. Use this alongside the Consultant Scoring Playbook and the Assessment Tool guide.

Key principles

  • The paid assessment (£495) is a standalone product — it is never credited against the managed service or onboarding. It stands alone commercially.
  • The lite assessment score is self-reported — it is never written to ConnectWise company fields. Only paid and ongoing assessments write to CW.
  • User count captured during the paid assessment drives managed service pricing automatically — Rewst calculates the correct product tier and pre-populates the proposal.
  • Every step that can be automated, is automated via Rewst. Manual steps are clearly labelled.

Phase 1 — Lead Generation · Step 1
Prospect Completes Lite Assessment
👤 Prospect — self-service✅ Live

A prospect arrives at littlebigtech.co.uk/assessment via marketing, referral, or sales outreach. Before accessing the assessment they complete a contact gate capturing name, email, company name, and phone number. They then complete the 30-question lite assessment — approximately 15 minutes.

ℹ️
Lite scores are self-reported and unvalidated

Lite assessment scores are never written to ConnectWise company fields. They exist in HubSpot and Pipedrive only. Only paid and ongoing assessments — verified by an LBT consultant — write to CW company records.

What is captured

  • Contact name, email, company, phone
  • Overall Technology Resilience Score (1–5)
  • Score per domain (10 domains)
  • Top 3 priority gaps
  • Critical gaps count (score = 1) and priority gaps count (score = 2)

Triggers

🟣 Prospect completes assessment🔷 Vercel API fires on submit⚡ Rewst webhook triggered
Phase 1 — Lead Generation · Step 2
Automation Fires — CRM, Emails, Teams Alert
⚡ Rewst — fully automated✅ Live

Immediately on assessment submission, the Vercel serverless API fires the Rewst webhook. Rewst orchestrates the following actions automatically — no manual intervention required.

What Rewst does

1
HubSpot contact upsert
Creates or updates the HubSpot contact with name, email, company, phone, overall score, maturity band, top risks, domain scores for Cyber, IAM, and BC/DR, critical gap count, and estimated ARR opportunity.
2
Pipedrive person + lead created
Creates a Pipedrive person record and a lead, linked to the contact. Lead value set to estimated ARR. Stage set to "New — Assessment Completed".
3
Email to prospect
Branded score report email sent to the prospect via Resend. Contains their overall score, domain breakdown, top 3 priority gaps, and a Calendly link to book a discovery call.
4
Sales alert in Teams
Alert fires to the LBT sales channel in Microsoft Teams. Contains: company name, contact name, overall score, top 3 risks, estimated ARR, and a direct link to the HubSpot contact record.

Triggers

⚡ Rewst webhook on submission⚡ HubSpot upsert⚡ Pipedrive lead created⚡ Resend email to prospect⚡ Teams sales channel alert
Phase 1 — Lead Generation · Step 3
Sales Response — Book Discovery Call
👤 Matt or Ryan⏱ SLA: within 2 hours of Teams alert

Matt or Ryan receives the Teams alert, opens the HubSpot contact to review the prospect's score and top gaps, and reaches out within 2 hours. The goal of this contact is to book a discovery call — not to sell the managed service, but to sell the paid assessment engagement.

How to personalise the outreach

Use the score data from the Teams alert to make the outreach specific. Examples:

  • "I can see your assessment flagged Business Continuity & DR as your highest-risk area — that's something we see a lot in [sector]. Worth a quick call to talk through what that actually means in practice?"
  • "Your Technology Resilience Score came in at 2.3 — most organisations your size are operating at 3.5+. We can walk you through exactly what's driving that gap in about 30 minutes."

Goal of this step

Book a discovery call (Step 4). The discovery call is where the paid assessment is sold. Do not try to sell the managed service at this stage.

Phase 2 — Assessment Sale · Step 4
Discovery Call — Sell the Paid Assessment
👤 Danny / Matt / Ryan

The discovery call uses the lite assessment score as the anchor. The goal is to explain the value of the paid assessment as a standalone commercial engagement and get agreement to proceed.

Call structure

1
Open with the score
"Your Technology Resilience Score of [X] places you below the benchmark for organisations your size — most are operating at 3.5+. That's not a criticism — it's actually a clear opportunity, and it's why I wanted to speak to you."
2
Walk through the top 2–3 gaps
Use the domain scores from HubSpot to pick the 2–3 most critical areas. Frame each as a business risk, not a technical gap. "Your backup position is your highest risk — without a tested recovery capability, a ransomware incident could be catastrophic."
3
Position the paid assessment
"What you've done today gives us the headline picture. The paid assessment is a 3–4 hour consultant-led session where we verify everything against your actual configuration and produce a full risk register, a phased roadmap, and a proposal for making these improvements. It's £495 and it gives you everything you need to make an informed decision about your technology investment."
4
Close to next step
Agreement to receive the assessment proposal, or verbal agreement to proceed. Do not send the proposal cold without a verbal yes — it should follow a committed conversation.

👤 Jack⏱ SLA: scheduled within 5 working days of project creation

Jack picks up the PSA project, contacts the client to find a suitable time for the 3–4 hour assessment session, and sends the pre-session email from the Hudu template.

Before the session

  • Open the consultant tool at lbt-assessment.vercel.app/consultant and complete the Client Setup tab before the session starts
  • Set the platform (M365 or Google Workspace) and operating model — these drive opt-out suggestions throughout the assessment
  • Note the client's user count and device count — required for managed service pricing

Phase 3 — Assessment Delivery · Step 8
Assessment Conducted Using Consultant Tool
👤 Jack🔧 Tool deploying

Jack conducts the full paid assessment using the consultant tool. The session takes 3–4 hours and covers all 10 domains across 30 controls, with platform-specific guidance for M365 or Google Workspace throughout.

Key things to capture

  • User count — total number of users. This drives managed service pricing.
  • Device count — total devices in scope. Referenced in the proposal.
  • Platform — M365 or Google Workspace (set in Setup tab)
  • Operating model — office / WFH / hybrid / serviced (drives opt-out suggestions)
  • Evidence — screenshots, policy references, dates for every control scored 3 or above
  • Opt-outs — any N/A, Third-Party, Deferred, or Risk Accepted controls with documented reasons
⚠️
If you cannot see it, you cannot score it above 2

Every score of 3 or above requires observable evidence captured during the session. Client reassurance is not evidence. Read the Scoring Playbook before your first session.

Phase 5 — Close & Onboarding · Step 15
Onboarding Delivery — Phase 1
👤 Jack + LBT team⏱ Phase 1 complete within 30 days of signing

Phase 1 items are delivered within 30 days. Every task in the onboarding project maps to a control in the assessment that scored below 2.5. On completion of Phase 1, Jack runs a score re-check in the consultant tool (Ongoing mode) to confirm the score has improved.

Phase 1 standard items

These are generated from the assessment. The exact list varies per client based on their scores, but typically includes:

  • Axient 360 immutable cloud backup — all M365 or Google Workspace workloads
  • MFA enforced via Conditional Access / Google 2SV — all users, all applications
  • Huntress full suite — MDR, ITDR, SAT across all endpoints
  • Proofpoint Essentials Advanced — email security + DMARC p=reject
  • ConnectWise PAM — privileged access management for admin accounts
  • JML process formalisation — leaver SLA ≤4 hours, ticket-tracked

90-day score re-check

When Phase 1 is complete, Jack opens the consultant tool in Ongoing mode, pre-populates the previous scores, updates only the controls that have changed, and submits. Rewst writes the updated scores to CW and generates a score re-check report emailed to the client.

Phase 5 — Close & Onboarding · Step 16
Quarterly QBR — Ongoing Growth Loop
👤 Jack + Danny + client⏱ Every 90 days✅ QBR mode built

Every 90 days, the Technology Resilience Score is reviewed and updated. This is not a passive check-in — it is a structured meeting that shows measurable progress, surfaces the next investment opportunities, and reinforces the LBT partnership narrative.

QBR workflow

1
Rewst reminder fires
Rewst checks LBT_Next_Assessment daily and fires a reminder to Jack when the date is reached. Jack opens the consultant tool in QBR mode.
2
Update current quarter scores
Jack updates scores for any controls that have changed since the last assessment. Previous scores are pre-populated. Only changed controls need updating.
3
Set client priorities
Before the meeting, ask the client what matters most right now — compliance, insurance, growth, resilience, risk reduction, or cost. Select up to 3 in the Client Priorities tab. This steers the action plan.
4
Run the meeting using the QBR Report tab
Show the score overview (QoQ delta), walk the prioritised action plan, show the score trajectory. Danny uses the remaining gaps to identify upsell opportunities naturally.
5
Submit — systems updated automatically
Rewst writes updated scores to CW, archives previous score, calculates delta. QBR report emailed to client. Next QBR date set to today + 90 days.
✅ Built: QBR mode live in consultant tool v3. Smart prioritisation engine with floor-first rule, client priority steering, quarter-on-quarter delta view, QBR report output.
